## 1. Overview

This design system channels the raw, exuberant energy of Nairobi's matatu culture – moving billboards that compete for attention through visual overload. Every surface is a canvas for hand-painted lettering, layered stickers, airbrushed portraits, and clashing high-saturation colors. The result is dense, asymmetric, and proudly imperfect, where scratches, paint drips, and UV fading are not flaws but honest marks of life on the road.

**Emotional tone:** High-energy, prideful, rebellious, celebratory.  
**Density:** Very dense – no negative space, every panel filled.  
**Rendering philosophy:** Emulate hand-painted and airbrushed techniques; accept asymmetry, wobble, and imperfection.  
**What makes it recognizable:** Circular route number badges, hard 3D drop shadows on lettering, layered decals, clashing neon-on-black, hazard-stripe bands, chrome trim lines.  
**What would break it:** Symmetrical layouts, pastel or muted colors, single font, clean vector perfection, consistent baselines.

## 8.5 Visual Effects

### 8.5.0 — Physical Material Model

Declares the top-level surface simulation governing the entire page canvas.
This is distinct from component-level textures (§8.5d) — it is the global rendering layer.

material-model:    collage — overlapping layers of hand-painted lettering, vinyl decals, stickers,
                    and chrome trim on a vehicle metal surface

global-filter:     saturate(1.15) contrast(1.1) — simulates the hyper-saturated visual punch
                    of matatu body graphics under equatorial sunlight, with vivid colors
                    and deep shadow contrast

global-overlay:    Dust & grain via body::after pseudo-element:
                    SVG feTurbulence baseFrequency=0.65 numOctaves=3 fractalNoise
                    blend: overlay, opacity-10, position:fixed, z-index:9999, pointer-events:none
                    Paint drip layer via separate body::before:
                    SVG feTurbulence baseFrequency=0.04 numOctaves=2 turbulence
                    blend: overlay, opacity-8, position:fixed, z-index:9998, pointer-events:none

rendering-flags:
  font-smoothing:  antialiased — display typefaces need crisp edges despite rough simulation
  image-rendering: auto — photographic airbrush portraits must render smoothly
  text-rendering:  auto

### 8.5a — Color Manipulation
No CSS filters are needed – the palette already handles saturation. A `hue-rotate` animation may be used on neon accents for strobe effects.

### 8.5b — Surface Layering (Backdrop Filters)
Used only for "chrome reflection" overlays on specific surface containers:
backdrop-filter: blur(4px) saturate(180%);
paired-fill: bg-white/5;

### 8.5c — Gradients & Glow

**Structural gradient (hazard-stripe separator):**
background-image: repeating-linear-gradient(45deg, #FFD700 0px, #FFD700 12px, #1A1A1A 12px, #1A1A1A 24px);
role: section divider
**Portrait airbrush gradient (soft radial):**
background-image: radial-gradient(ellipse at 40% 40%, #FF1493 0%, #0F52BA 50%, #1A1A1A 100%);
role: image overlay for crew portraits
**Neon glow (midnight mode only):**
text-shadow: 0 0 8px #39FF14, 0 0 20px #39FF14;
animation: pulse-glow 1.5s ease-in-out infinite;

### 8.5d — Texture & Noise Simulation

**Paint Drip / Drip Noise:**
technique: SVG feTurbulence (organic drip pattern)
parameters: baseFrequency: 0.04, numOctaves: 2, type: turbulence
surface: full-page backdrop (subtle, opacity-8)
intensity: moderate
blend: mix-blend-mode: overlay, opacity-10
color: monochrome (black)
animation: none
tailwind-approximation: no Tailwind equivalent; use `backdrop: url(data:...)`

**Scratch & Wear (thin lines):**

**Dust & Grain:**

## 10. Layout

**Spacing cadence:** Dense – use `gap-2` between all adjacent elements, `gap-4` between sections. Avoid white space over 16px.

**Grid tendency:** Asymmetric – left and right halves can have different content weights. Avoid equal-column layouts. Use `grid-cols-2` with different column spans (e.g., left 2/3, right 1/3) or `flex` with `justify-between` and uneven width.

**Density:** Maximum – fill available width. No horizontal padding beyond `p-6` at page edges.

**Section separation:** Use hazard-stripe bands (8.5c) between major sections. Minor sections separated by a single chrome trim bar (2px).

**Alignment philosophy:** Text is left-aligned or centered, never justified. Items within a section may be off-center to mimic hand-painted placement.

**Breakpoint behavior (375px):**
- Stack two-column layout to single column
- Reduce padding to `p-4` on page edges
- Route number circle shrinks to `w-12 h-12`
- Hazard stripe separators become thinner (`h-1`)
- Chrome trim bars become `h-1`

**Breakpoint behavior (768px):**
- Two-column returns but asymmetrical (left 60%, right 40%)
- Hazard stripe bands remain `h-2`
- Route number circle `w-14 h-14`
- Sticker chips become `text-xs` (no change)

## 11. Design System Notes

### 11a. Use Constraints

**Appropriate for:** High-energy, attention-grabbing contexts – transport dashboards, event landing pages, music player modals, game HUDs, promotional banners for streetwear or Afrobeats brands, any application targeting attention through visual excess.

**Wrong for:** Calm productivity tools (spreadsheets, notes), healthcare interfaces, banking dashboards, any context requiring high legibility and low cognitive load, government forms, minimal editorial design.

### 11b. Prompt Phrases

- "Call up the chaos of a Nairobi matatu — layer clashing saturated colors and irregular hand-painted lettering."
- "Use ALL CAPS text with a hard 3D drop shadow in contrasting color, offset 1.5px right and down, no blur."
- "Add a hazard-stripe separator using a repeating diagonal gradient of gold and black."
- "Cover every panel with overlapping sticker chips, each slightly rotated, varying in opacity."
- "Place the primary number inside a circle with thick white border, rotate it 3 degrees off-axis."
- "Emulate chrome trim with a silver gradient bar, flanked by thin borders of lighter silver."
- "Incorporate a single high-contrast portrait with an airbrushed radial gradient overlay."
- "Scatter paint-drip texture across the background at low opacity to simulate wear."

### 11c. Do / Avoid Block

Each rule is a discrete triplet: the governing rule stated plainly, then the correct visual description, then the incorrect visual description. Minimum 4 rules, maximum 8.

rule: Colors must be maximum saturation — no pastels, no muted tones.
do:   Use fully saturated primary (#FF1744), secondary (#00E676), and accent (#FFD700). No desaturation allowed anywhere.
avoid:Muted tones, desaturated palettes, faded pastels.

rule: Text must be ALL CAPS in all contexts without exception.
do:   All text — labels, values, buttons, decorative elements — set in uppercase, font-bold, tracking-wider.
avoid:Mixed case, sentence case, lowercase text, or inconsistently cased use.

rule: Display and headline text must carry a hard 3D drop shadow with zero blur.
do:   Apply text-shadow: 1.5px 1.5px 0 #1A1A1A (or analogous high-contrast color) to all headline text. No blur.
avoid:Clean flat text, text shadows with blur, soft umbra shadows.

rule: Elements must overlap with slight rotation to simulate hand-painted asymmetry.
do:   Apply transform: rotate(±2–5deg) to overlapping stickers, route circles, cards, and panels. Each element rotated individually.
avoid:Aligned grids, consistent margins, perfectly horizontal or vertical alignment, uniform rotation.

rule: Each major section must include at least one hazard-stripe separator.
do:   Use a full-width repeating diagonal linear gradient (45deg, #FFD700 0 8px, #1A1A1A 8px 16px) at least 4px tall.
avoid:Single-color dividers, fine lines thinner than 2px, rounded corners, absence of stripe separators.

rule: Panels must be densely layered with multiple overlapping motifs, each rotated randomly, leaving no empty space over 16px.
do:   Fill every panel with overlapping sticker chips, decals, portraits, and hazard stripes. Gap between elements ≤ 16px, rotations ±2–5deg.
avoid:Aligned grids, uniform rotation, empty space over 16px, single focal point, sparse layouts.

rule: All surfaces must have texture (grain, scratches, drips) and borders must use chrome silver or gold trim — never single-color flat borders.
do:   Apply SVG feTurbulence grain at 8–12% opacity on all surfaces. Borders use chrome linear-gradient (#A0A0A0 → #C0C0C0 → #F0F0F0 → #C0C0C0) or gold (#FFD700) with thin outlines.
avoid:Vector-perfect edges, no texture, clean smooth surfaces, single-color flat borders, rounded corners.

rule: Stickers and circular elements must have randomized rotation — no two identically aligned.
do:   Apply rotate-(random ±2deg) to sticker chips and route number circles, ensuring each element has a unique rotation.
avoid:Perfectly horizontal or vertical alignment of any sticker or circle.
